**Step 3 — Add section-scoped regression-guard tests to `src/emails/sendUpdate/SendUpdate.test.ts`.**

The new tests slice the rendered HTML by section eyebrow and assert that within each section's slice, zero `<td ... align="right">` and zero `<td ... align="center">` cells exist. It deliberately ignores:

- `<table align="center">` wrapper attributes emitted by react-email's `<Container>` / `<Section>` / `<Row>` primitives (the email-safe centering pattern — out of D-7 scope)
- `<td align="right">` cells inside ReviewItems' due-label column (legitimate, out of D-7 scope; ReviewItems renders BEFORE Procurement, so its cells fall UPSTREAM of `>Procurement</p>` and are excluded automatically by the slice start sentinel)

**Slice-strategy notes (read before the code below):**

- The Milestones slice (`>Milestones</p>` to `>Procurement</p>`) is bounded by the next-section eyebrow and works because Milestones renders immediately before Procurement in `SendUpdate.tsx` (lines 177–178).
- The Procurement slice ends at end-of-string (`html.length`), NOT at the ReviewItems eyebrow. Reason: ReviewItems renders BEFORE Milestones (`SendUpdate.tsx` line 174), so `>For your review</p>` sits UPSTREAM of `>Procurement</p>` and `indexOf(">For your review</p>", procStart)` returns `-1`. End-of-string is safe because Procurement is the last enumerated content section before the CTA + Footer chrome, and that chrome was verified at plan time (against `__snapshots__/SendUpdate.test.ts.snap` across all four Procurement-bearing fixtures: `full`, `mixedSubLines`, `noBody`, `noReviewItems`) to contain zero `<td align="right">` and zero `<td align="center">` cells. The CTA section emits `<table align="center">` wrappers from react-email primitives plus an inline-styled `<a>` button (no `<td>` cells with right/center alignment); the footer is `<table align="center">`-wrapped with no `<td>` right/center cells.
- **Future-proofing implication:** if a later phase inserts a new section between Procurement and the CTA, OR adds a `<td align="right|center">` cell anywhere in the CTA / Footer chrome, this assertion will flag it as zero-becomes-nonzero. Treat such a flag as a regression worth investigating: re-scope the Procurement slice to a tighter sentinel (likely `</a>` of the CTA button or the opening of `<EmailFooter>`) only after confirming the new cell is intentional and outside D-7 scope.
